An Analysis On the Use of Barbecue Sauce Post Ice Age

Author: A. L. Acharya

Publication Date: March 2023

What is BBQ sauce?
Barbecue, often abbreviated as BBQ, sauce is often used for basting, marinading, a condiment or for topping meat including pork or beef ribs and chicken. But BBQ sauce is so much more than a sauce. How is it that this sticky, brown sauce has captivated the hearts of so many people across the world? Where did it start and how far will it go?
What are the main ingredients?

According to Allrecipes user JRNEUMILLER, BBQ sauce is made using ketchup, brown sugar, vinegar, water, various spices and seasonings including Worcestershire sauce, paprika, dry mustard, salt and black pepper, and dash of hot sauce (allrecipes, 2023). However, the sauce has much simpler origins: Moss (2010) states that early homemade BBQ sauce was made with vinegar, salt and pepper.

Where did it originate?

According to the Tennessee State Museum, the origins of barbecue sauce trace back to 1698 when Père Labat, a Dominican missionary, saw French West Indies chefs barbecuing meat and using lime juice and hot peppers; they continue that it may have started in Africa, where lemon and lime was often used to marinade or baste meat (Rothbarth, 2022). High Jr (2022) states there are actually four variations of the sauce that have evolved over the years, the oldest being simply vinegar and pepper. Other variants include mustard, Light Tomato and Heavy Tomato. Vinegar and mustard-based sauces became popular in the American South in the 1800s, when more people started experimenting with barbecue and as of today, America is well renowned for its love of barbecue (Rothbarth, 2022). As a result, barbecue sauce produces high sales in supermarkets. Moran (2005) observed in a study of condiments that 81% of participants preferred BBQ sauce as an accompaniment to ribs, chicken nuggets and chicken.

Is there any cultural significance?

Barbecuing as a cooking style has always been seen as a community, shared experience since it started (Sugar, 2021). It holds cultural significance in the way it has developed and changed over the centuries: Spanish colonisers brought over pigs to the Indigenous Peoples of the Caribbean and Florida Regions in the ‘New World’, which is now the Southern United States, who in turn started incorporating the animal in their slow cooking methods. The colonisers were met with a wide range of flavours spanning various continents: wine, vinegar and salt from Spain, sugar and molasses from the Caribbean, chilli peppers from Central America and tomatoes from South America (Sugar.org, 2021). The condiment is particularly special to North and South Carolina, where the vinegar and mustard base was predominantly used (High Jr, 2022). South Carolina has one of the largest barbecuing communities, so it is only right it is home to three of the four barbecue sauces used in America (High Jr, 2022).

Conclusion and Acknowledgements

In conclusion, barbecue sauce is an extremely popular condiment, beloved to many for its sweet yet smoky taste and sticky texture. It adds a punch of flavour to smoked meats or even to just dip your chicken nuggets in.
